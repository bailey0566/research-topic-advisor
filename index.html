Research Topic Builder - Step 2: Define the Problem

How to Write a Research Problem Statement

Use this proven formula for clarity and impact:

In [context], [who] is experiencing [problem], which leads to [impact].

What goes in each part?

  • Context: Setting/environment (school type, grade level, subject area)
  • Who: Specific group affected (students, teachers, administrators)
  • Problem: Observable issue or challenge
  • Impact: Negative consequence or outcome

Writing Tips

  • Be specific and measurable
  • Use neutral, objective language
  • Focus on observable behaviors/outcomes
  • Connect problem to real consequences
  • Avoid blame or assumptions

Examples of Strong Problem Statements

✓ Academic Achievement:
"In high school science classes, students from underrepresented minority groups demonstrate lower performance on standardized assessments, which limits their access to advanced science courses and STEM career opportunities."
✓ Student Engagement:
"In middle school mathematics classrooms, students show decreasing participation in collaborative problem-solving activities, which hinders the development of critical thinking and mathematical reasoning skills."
✓ Teacher Development:
"In rural school districts, new teachers receive insufficient mentoring and professional development support, which contributes to higher turnover rates and inconsistent instructional quality."
✓ Equity & Inclusion:
"In elementary schools serving diverse populations, English language learners are not receiving adequate differentiated instruction, which affects their academic progress and social integration with peers."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too vague: "Students struggle in math" → "Students score below grade level on multiplication fluency assessments"
  • No impact: "Teachers use outdated methods" → "...which reduces student engagement and learning outcomes"
  • Blame language: "Bad teachers cause problems" → "Teachers lack training in evidence-based strategies"
  • Unresearchable: "Everyone should be happy" → "Students report lower satisfaction with school climate surveys"

Research Readiness Checklist

  • ☐ Problem is observable and measurable
  • ☐ Specific population and context identified
  • ☐ Clear connection between problem and impact
  • ☐ Feasible to study within time/resources available
  • ☐ Ethical considerations addressed
